Predictions of
ICU Mortality

Will the patient survive the hospitalisation stay?

The development of methods for prediction of mortality rates in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) has been motivated by the need to to increase treatment effectiveness, improved care in ICU, and functional discharge to post-acute care facilities.

This project is obtained from the PhysioNet/CinC Challenge 2012. The main aim is to develop a solution for patient-specific prediction of in-hospital mortality that serves as a decision support for medical practitioners and medical care givers.

Data

The data used in this project consist of records from 4,000 ICU stays. All patients were adults who were admitted for a wide variety of reasons - cardiac, medical, surgical, or trauma.
Up to 42 variables were recorded at least once during the first 48 hours:


General Descriptors

RecordID Integer
Age (years)
Height (cm)
ICUtype (1: Coronary Care, 2: Cardiac Surgery Recovery,
3: Medical ICU, or 4: Surgical ICU)
Gender (0: female, or 1: male)

Outcome-related descriptors

SAPS-I score between 0 to 163
SOFA score between 0 to 4
Length of stay (days)
Survival (days)
In-hospital death (0: survivor, or 1: died in-hospital)
