in-hospital mortality prediction serves as a decision support for healthcare providers

The development of methods for prediction of mortality rates in the ICU has been motivated by the need to to increase treatment effectiveness, improved care in ICU, and functional discharge to post-acute care facilities.

This project is obtained from the PhysioNet/CinC Challenge 2012. The aim is to develop a solution for patient-specific prediction of in-hospital mortality can serve as a decision support for medical practitioners and medical care givers.

Data

The data used in this project consist of records from 4,000 ICU stays. All patients were adults who were admitted for a wide variety of reasons to cardiac, medical, surgical, and trauma ICUs.

Up to 42 variables were recorded at least once during the first 48 hours:

General Descriptors: Age, gender, weight, and type of ICU

Time Series Variables: Temperature, heart rate, diastolic/systolic arterial blood pressure and lab tests

*for which multiple observations may be available.

Outcome-related descriptors:

  • SAPS-I score (Le Gall et al., 1984)
  • SOFA score (Ferreira et al., 2001)
  • Length of stay (days)
  • Survival (days)
  • In-hospital death (0: survivor, or 1: died in-hospital)
